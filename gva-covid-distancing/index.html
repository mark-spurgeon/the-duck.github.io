How can we keep safe social distances in Geneva ?

With its 21k inhabitants per square metre, Geneva is one of the most densely populated cities in Europe. For that reason among others, it has seen many cases of COVID-19 infections.

As of April 18th 2020, the situation in Switzerland is encouraging, prompting the government to slowly relax social distancing measures in the next two months.

However, with a growing number of cases and no vaccine to this day, we need to keep avoiding physical contact with each other. It is crucial that during our trips outside, to shop, run or have a quick walk, we must keep a safe distance of 2 metres.

The dense nature of the city makes it hard to respect this rule, and there's been little help from Geneva's local authorities to support citizen accomplishing that duty, but it's not too late to take action. This map aims at the showing concentrations of shops where free space is missing. It also points at measures to help temporarily free up space for citizen. In the next weeks, as more shops will open doors, these measures will be necessary to keep everybody safe.
