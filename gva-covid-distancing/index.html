How to keep safe social distances in Geneva, Switzerland

With its 21k inhabitants per square metre, Geneva is one of the most densely populated cities in Europe. For that reason among others, it has seen many cases of COVID-19 infections.

As of April 18th 2020, the situation in Switzerland is encouraging, prompting the government to slowly relax social distancing measures in the next two months.

However, with a growing number of cases and no vaccine to this day, we need to keep avoiding contact with each other. It is crucial that during our trips outside, to shop, run or have a quick walk, we must keep a safe distance from other people (2 metres).

There's been little action from Geneva's local authorities, but it's not too late. This map aims at showing concentrations of shops where free space is missing. It also points at potential measures to help free up space for queuers. In the next weeks, as more shops will open doors, these measures may well be necessary to keep everyone safe.
